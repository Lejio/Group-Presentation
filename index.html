Phased Construction

Image from "Is Phased Construction Right For Your Commercial Project?", Emerald Inc.

There is a lower initial investment as the project is split up into smaller, more affordable pieces. (Merrill, 2020) Problems that occur during the first few phases can be worked out before continuing on to other phases, allowing time and planning to make changes. (Merrill, 2020)

Phased Construction

Image from "Is Phased Construction Right For Your Commercial Project?", Emerald Inc.

University of Minnesota utilizes phased construction to allow parts of buildings to be completely functional while other parts are under construction, which minimizes displacement and disruption to a student’s day. (Procedures for phased construction projects: UMN University Health & Safety) There is a lower initial investment cost, and you don't have to pay for the whole project at once, making it easier to budget. Breaking the project up into small pieces results in breaking the overall cost up into small pieces, making it more manageable than paying for the whole project all at once. (Beran, 2021)

Flexible Construction

Image from Horwitz & Associates.

In order to reduce the hazards of construction for students and members of the community, having a flexible schedule for construction and having a 24/7 walkway for students can level off the cramped walkways and limit the disruption of traffic, as well as reduce potential threats.
